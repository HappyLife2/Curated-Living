# Curated Living — Brand & Business Foundation

> *"Hand me the keys. In 2–4 weeks, I hand back a furnished, styled, rent-ready home."*

**Founder:** Melissa Madonna Fernandes — Senior FF&E & Interior Designer, ~10 yrs (Dubai)
**Status:** New venture, foundation stage · Doc v1 · 2026-06-13
**Relationship to existing brand:** A **separate business** from Atelier Madonna (her full-service luxury interior design studio). Same founder, different funnel. See §2.

---

## 1. Executive summary

Curated Living is a **designer-led turnkey furnishing & styling service** for Dubai property
investors and owners. The client is not buying *design* — they're buying **convenience, speed,
and a finished apartment**. Give us the keys to an empty unit; in 2–4 weeks we hand back a
furnished, styled, photography-ready, tenant-ready home, having handled sourcing, procurement,
delivery, installation, and styling end to end.

It is deliberately **not** a slow, bespoke interior-design process and **not** a cheap furniture-pack
shop. It sits in the gap between the two: the taste and judgement of a senior FF&E designer,
delivered through a fast, packaged, repeatable system.

**Why this model wins for a solo founder** (from the strategy analysis):
- Low time-per-project (days of design effort, weeks of revenue) → highest return-on-time of any
  interior-services model.
- No drawings, contractors, authority approvals, site labour, or large working capital.
- Scales through **standardised packages**, not bespoke custom work.
- Plays directly to Melissa's strengths: FF&E expertise, vendor knowledge, styling, luxury
  residential background.

**Target unit economics:** ≈ AED 8,000 profit per apartment × 4–8 apartments/month
= **AED 32,000–64,000/month**, solo, with low overhead.

## 2. How this differs from Atelier Madonna

Both are Melissa's. They are **not the same business** and should not share a website, funnel,
or positioning. This brand targets the *transactional* end of the market; Atelier Madonna targets
the *bespoke* end.

| | **Atelier Madonna** (existing) | **Curated Living** (this venture) |
|---|---|---|
| Sells | A bespoke design vision | Convenience + speed + a finished home |
| Buyer | Owners who want their space *designed* | Investors, overseas owners, Airbnb/holiday-home operators, landlords, busy buyers |
| Timeline | Months (concept → drawings → site) | **2–4 weeks** |
| Scope | Full interior design, documentation, FF&E, site | **FF&E + procurement + styling only** — no drawings, no contractors, no renovation |
| Pricing | Design fees / % of project | Fixed fee + procurement fee, or turnkey margin |
| Emotional register | Artisan, warm, slow luxury | Effortless, decisive, "done-for-you" |
| Brand feel | Warm Earthy Atelier (cream/clay/sage) | Crisp editorial premium (see §11) — intentionally distinct |

**Cross-referral is a feature:** a Curated Living investor who later wants a bespoke home → Atelier
Madonna. An Atelier Madonna enquiry that's really "just furnish my rental fast" → Curated Living.
Two brands, one founder, one referral loop.

## 3. The problem & the opportunity

Dubai has a large, continuous flow of people who own an empty apartment and need it furnished —
fast, well, and without effort:

- They don't want to deal with 8 different suppliers, quotations, deliveries, and installation.
- They don't have time for a months-long design process.
- They need the unit **rent-ready** (often Airbnb / short-let / long-let) to start earning.

The market today is split into two unsatisfying options:

1. **Furniture-package companies** — generic packs, little design input, run by salespeople,
   compete on price.
2. **Full-service interior designers** — expensive, slow, far too much process for a rental unit.

**The gap:** a *designer-led*, *packaged*, *fast* furnishing service. "Designer taste, delivered
like a product." That's Curated Living.

## 6. Services & packages

Four offers — three **fixed-price packs** (the repeatable profit engine) and one **bespoke** service
(the differentiator). Every pack covers Studio → 4 BHK.

### 1. Essential — *the standard pack*
For: investors & landlords furnishing to let.
- Full furniture across every room
- Lighting, soft furnishings & essentials
- Procurement, delivery & installation
- Styling & tenant-ready handover
- Fixed price — no surprises

### 2. Premium — *elevated furnishing*
For: owners & premium rentals who want a step up.
- Everything in Essential, plus:
- Higher-end furniture & decorative lighting
- Artwork & curated accessories
- Layered textiles & soft furnishings
- Full turnkey handover

### 3. Holiday Home — *short-let / Airbnb, guest-ready*
For: Airbnb & holiday-home operators. **Premium + AED 4,000 per size.** The differentiator: it
**includes basic OS&E as standard** — the durable supplies a short-let must have to host (cutlery,
crockery, glassware, cookware, one set of linens & towels) — so it's genuinely guest-ready out of the box.
- Premium-level furnishing & broad-appeal styling
- **Basic OS&E included** (durable: cutlery, crockery, linens, kitchen kit)
- Photography-ready setup (listing photos)
- Optional re-style / refresh cycles

> **Optional add-on (any pack) — Electronics Pack:** TV, microwave, toaster, kettle, hairdryer, iron and
> the everyday electronics a home needs, sourced and set up with the furniture. Quoted per unit.

### 4. Bespoke — *fully custom, for the home you'll live in*
For: owner-occupiers who want a personal scheme, not a pack. **This is the differentiator** — the
service most furnishing companies don't offer.
- A custom scheme designed to your taste
- Fixed design fee by unit size
- Furniture billed at cost — fully transparent
- Procurement, delivery, install & styling

**Scope guardrails (what Curated Living does NOT do):** technical drawings, authority approvals,
joinery/contracting, renovation, MEP — those belong to Atelier Madonna / fit-out firms. Holding
this line is what keeps the model fast and solo-able.

## 7. Pricing model

**Fixed, all-in prices by unit size** for the three packs (no budget guesswork), and a transparent
**design-fee model** for Bespoke.

### Fixed-price packs (AED, furniture included)
| Pack | Studio | 1 BHK | 2 BHK | 3 BHK | 4 BHK |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Essential** | 15,000 | 17,000 | 20,000 | 23,000 | 26,000 |
| **Premium** | 19,000 | 21,000 | 24,000 | 27,000 | 30,000 |
| **Holiday Home** | 23,000 | 25,000 | 28,000 | 31,000 | 34,000 |

- Premium = Essential **+ AED 4,000** at every size.
- Holiday Home = **Premium + AED 4,000** at every size, and **includes basic OS&E** as standard
  (durable supplies the short-let needs to host).
- **Optional add-on, any pack — Electronics Pack** (TV, microwave, toaster, kettle, hairdryer, iron,
  etc.), quoted per unit.

### Bespoke (custom — different structure)
- **Fixed design fee by unit size:** Studio 5,000 · 1 BHK 7,000 · 2 BHK 10,000 · 3 BHK 13,000 · 4 BHK 16,000
- **+ Furniture at actuals** — billed transparently at cost.
- **+ 5–10% management fee** on total furnishing value.

> **Why fixed pricing wins:** it removes the single biggest hesitation for an investor (open-ended
> cost) and makes the offer instantly comparable and easy to say yes to — while Bespoke captures the
> higher-touch owner-occupier the pack model can't serve.

> **Open item:** the **Electronics Pack** add-on is priced per unit (no fixed figure set yet). Basic OS&E
> is included in the Holiday Home price.

Margins are high because there's **no inventory, no construction liability, no site labour**, and

flow and throughput**, not delivery capability — which is why §9 is the most important section.

## 9. Go-to-market — the lead engine (most important section)

> The biggest challenge will **not** be design or delivery. It will be **getting leads.**

For this business, relationships beat marketing. **One referral partner who consistently sends
investor clients is worth more than months of Instagram.** Build the partner network as
deliberately as the website:

**Priority referral partners:**
- **Real-estate brokers / agents** (esp. resale & investor-focused)
- **Property managers**
- **Airbnb / short-let management companies**
- **Holiday-home operators**
- **Developer sales agents** (Emaar etc. — they meet buyers at handover)

**Partner offer:** a clean referral commission + a frictionless "send me the keys" handoff, so the
broker looks good to *their* client by solving the furnishing problem for them.

**Secondary channels:**
- Website optimised for investor intent + SEO/GEO ("furnish apartment Dubai to rent", "rental-ready furnishing Dubai", community-specific pages: Dubai Hills, Creek Harbour, Marina).
- Instagram: before/empty → after/styled reels; "keys to rent-ready in 21 days" format.
- Listing-photo quality as marketing collateral (every finished unit is content).

## 10. Operations — how it works (the 2–4 week system)

**Client-facing flow (the product):**
1. **Keys & brief** — budget, timeline, rental target (long-let / Airbnb), style direction.
2. **Furnishing concept** — package selection + curated board (days, not weeks).
3. **Procurement** — trade sourcing, orders, supplier coordination.
4. **Delivery & install** — scheduled, supervised.
5. **Styling** — final layering, accessories, photo-ready.
6. **Handover** — tenant/guest-ready; listing photos.

**Solo workflow + what to outsource** (keep overhead low, scale throughput):
- Melissa keeps: client/partner relationships, concept & selections, styling, key decisions.
- Outsource as revenue allows: **delivery/install labour (per-job)**, **a VA** (quotes, supplier
  chasing, scheduling, invoicing), and a **photographer** (per shoot). Clients still see one point
  of contact.
- Systematise: a vetted **supplier roster** with trade terms, **package templates** (SKUs per tier),
  and a repeatable **project checklist** — this is what turns bespoke effort into a product.

## 11. Brand identity direction (proposal — not yet locked)

Curated Living must look **distinct from Atelier Madonna's warm-earthy artisan world**. Where
Atelier Madonna is slow, tactile, and crafted, Curated Living should feel **effortless, editorial,
and decisive** — a premium real-estate-lifestyle brand, not a craft atelier.

**Proposed direction — "Crisp Editorial Premium":**
- **Palette:** clean warm-white / off-white base, soft greige neutrals, with one confident accent
  (e.g. deep ink/charcoal or a muted brass/bronze) — restrained, not earthy.
- **Type:** a refined **Didone serif** for the wordmark (high contrast, optically kerned), paired
  with a clean neutral sans for UI/body — restraint over decoration, set on a disciplined grid.
  *(Consistent with the house logo standard: Didone serif + optical kerning + restraint + grid;
  avoid humanist fonts and gimmick marks.)*
- **Logo:** wordmark-led. **"CURATED LIVING"** with **"by Melissa Fernandes"** as a quiet
  supporting line — the brand stands alone, her name provides the trust. No literal house/sofa icon.
- **Photography:** the hero of the brand — real finished apartments, natural light, editorial
  styling. The work *is* the marketing.
- **Voice:** confident, concise, outcome-led ("rent-ready in weeks"), never salesy.

> Decision needed: confirm this direction (or steer toward something cooler/more minimal) before
> any logo or site work.
